About the Programme

Building Back Better Across Ukraine

EU4Reconstruction is a flagship initiative under the Ukraine Facility, channelling European solidarity into measurable outcomes for war-affected regions.

The programme operates across four interconnected funding streams — covering immediate shelter, critical infrastructure, energy resilience, and municipal capacity — ensuring that no aspect of community recovery is left unaddressed.

Working alongside Ukrainian national and local authorities, civil society organisations, and specialised EU agencies, EU4Reconstruction embeds accountability, sustainability, and inclusivity into every project it supports.

Funding Streams

Four Pillars of Reconstruction

Each stream targets a distinct priority area, together providing comprehensive support for community recovery.

🏠
Stream 1

Emergency Shelter & Housing

Rapid repair and reconstruction of residential buildings, temporary accommodation, and social housing for displaced families.

🏗️
Stream 2

Critical Infrastructure

Restoration of roads, bridges, water networks, and public buildings essential for civilian life and economic recovery.

⚡
Stream 3

Energy Resilience

Investment in decentralised energy systems, district heating restoration, and renewable capacity to reduce dependence on centralised grids.

🏛️
Stream 4

Municipal Capacity

Technical assistance and institutional support to strengthen local governance, procurement, and service delivery capacity.
